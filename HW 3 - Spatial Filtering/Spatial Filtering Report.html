Linear Smoothing Filter (Averaging Filter)

The result is a little blurry. The noise is gone, but so is the definition!

Nonlinear Smoothing Filter (Gaussian Filter)

The box blur (Averaging Filter) achieved about the same results as this one. The corners of the white square are a bit more rounded than the result from the box blur.

Nonlinear (order-Statistics) Filter (Median Filter)

This version has a notable posterization effect; if one zooms in, one will notice areas of color resembling rice paddies. The white square appears to have had its corners trimmed at 45-degree angles.

Second Derivative Sharpening Filter (Laplacian Filter)

I added the intensity-scaled second derivative of the noise-reduced image to the original image. The result is a crisper image. The lines on the basketball and the horizontal lines on the back wall have become more defined.

First Derivative Filter for Edge Detection (Sobel Filter)

The image was median filtered before performing the Sobel filters, in order to avoid enhancing noise. The X and Y filters brought out the horizontal and vertical features of the image, and the combined image enhances the entire contour. It could have been blurred more broadly; notice that the right-hand player's entire arm is filled in.
