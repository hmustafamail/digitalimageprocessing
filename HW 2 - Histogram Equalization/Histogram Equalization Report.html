Running Histogram Equalization on Types of Images

Dark Image

The equalized image feels much more clear than the original. The bricks have become far more visible, and the hairline, especially near the parting, has become more clearly contoured.

The original histogram clearly drops off to the right-hand side, while the equalized histogram is far more level.

Bright Image

In the bright image, Fabio's face appears washed out. Amazingly, in the equalized image, the cheekbone structure, nose, lips, and lower eyelids are recovered, and much more texture is visible in the hair.

The original histogram has more weight on the right-hand side, and sort of a bowl structure, while the equalized histogram is more filled in and even.

Low Contrast Image

The difference here is striking. The low contrast image is reminiscent of a smoke-filled room. In the equalized image, the bricks and facial features are far more visible, and it overall feels more "clear".

While the original histogram is tight and constricted, the equalized histogram is filled out.

High Contrast Image

While some shadowing on Fabio's face has become more visible, unlike in previous examples, the bricks have not sprung into clarity. Additionally, the facial shadowing has the appearance of a sponge. Rather than becoming more natural to the eye, the equalized image remains poster-like.

The "equalized" histogram, far from being levelled, has somehow become more tight and restricted. This one appears to have backfired.
